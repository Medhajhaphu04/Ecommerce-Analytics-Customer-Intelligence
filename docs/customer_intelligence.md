# Customer Intelligence & Retention Analytics

## Objective

The objective of this phase was to build a customer intelligence layer using SQL analytics.

The analysis focuses on understanding customer purchasing behavior, customer value, retention patterns, acquisition trends, and revenue contribution. The resulting insights will support future machine learning tasks such as customer segmentation, customer lifetime value prediction, and sales forecasting.

---

## SQL Concepts Used

* Common Table Expressions (CTEs)
* Window Functions
* NTILE()
* LAG()
* CASE WHEN
* Aggregate Functions
* Customer-Level Feature Engineering

---

## Business Questions

### Customer Behavior

* How many customers are repeat buyers?
* How many customers purchase only once?
* How frequently do customers place orders?

### Customer Value

* Which customers generate the highest revenue?
* How is revenue distributed across the customer base?
* Can customers be segmented based on spending behavior?

### Business Growth

* How does revenue change over time?
* How many new customers are acquired each month?
* Is average order value increasing or decreasing?

### Product Performance

* Which product categories contribute the highest revenue?

---

## Analysis Performed

### Repeat Customer Analysis

Customer purchase frequency was analyzed to distinguish one-time buyers from repeat customers.

This analysis provides an indication of customer retention and long-term platform engagement.

---

### Customer Revenue Analysis

Total revenue generated by each customer was calculated using order and payment information.

The objective was to identify high-value customers and evaluate revenue concentration across the customer base.

---

### Revenue Concentration Analysis

Customers were grouped into revenue-based deciles using window functions.

This analysis helps determine whether a small percentage of customers contributes a disproportionately large share of total revenue.

---

### Monthly Revenue Analysis

Monthly revenue trends were calculated using transaction history.

The analysis provides insights into business growth patterns and seasonality.

---

### Month-over-Month Growth Analysis

Revenue growth rates were calculated using the LAG() window function.

This allows the identification of periods of expansion and decline.

---

### Customer Acquisition Analysis

The first purchase date of each customer was used to calculate monthly customer acquisition trends.

This metric helps evaluate marketplace growth.

---

### Average Order Value Analysis

Average order value was calculated across monthly periods to understand changes in customer spending behavior.

---

### Customer Segmentation

Customers were classified into:

* VIP Customers
* Premium Customers
* Regular Customers

based on total revenue generated.

This segmentation will be reused in future customer intelligence and retention models.

---

### Revenue Category Analysis

Revenue contribution was analyzed across product categories.

The objective was to identify categories driving the majority of business revenue.

---

### Customer Feature Engineering

A customer-level analytical dataset was created containing:

* Frequency
* Monetary Value
* Average Order Value
* Last Purchase Date

These features form the foundation of future machine learning workflows.
